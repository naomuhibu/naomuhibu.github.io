Thesis Prototype

Autonomous Hardware Control and Multi-Sensory Anomaly Detection in Precision Agriculture

"Integrating Edge AI, Acoustic Sensor Networks, and 3D Digital Twins to automate greenhouse environments and maximize Land Use Efficiency."

Research Objectives:

  • Multi-Modal Anomaly Detection: Fusing spatial audio (microphone arrays) and depth-sensing vision to identify equipment anomalies and crop health issues in real-time.
  • Autonomous Hardware Control: Implementing edge-based microcontrollers for self-regulating fail-safes (e.g., autonomous ventilation) to minimize farmer physical load.
  • Immersive Digital Twins: Utilizing Unity-based 3D visualization and Spatial Audio feedback to provide an intuitive, remote greenhouse monitoring interface.
  • Cloud-Native Scalability: Designing a low-latency, multi-cloud architecture for robust regional agricultural deployment and resource optimization.

Sustainable Residential Land Use: Multi-Agent Autonomous Smart Homes

"Leveraging Multi-Agent Systems, Acoustic-Visual Sensor Fusion, and 3D Digital Twins to automate residential environments and optimize energy consumption."

Research Objectives:

  • Multi-Agent Coordination: Developing autonomous AI agents that seamlessly communicate to manage HVAC, appliances, and security based on real-time resident context.
  • Privacy-Preserving Context Awareness: Utilizing OAK-D-Lite for skeletal pose estimation (fall detection) and microphone arrays for acoustic anomaly recognition without recording raw visual/audio data.
  • Immersive 3D Interfaces: Creating a Unity-based Digital Twin of the home for intuitive remote management, enhanced by spatial audio alerts.
  • Energy Optimization: Minimizing energy waste through predictive climate and lighting control, directly contributing to sustainable urban infrastructure.
